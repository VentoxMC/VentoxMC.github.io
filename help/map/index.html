The Ventox Map

The Ventox Universe is made of several different worlds that consist of different landmarks and functions. Some of these worlds reset each "season" whereas other worlds never change. In the guide below, each element of the Ventox Map and a Ventox Season will be explored.

Seasons

In order to ensure a fresh gameplay experience on Ventox, we have split the calendar year into "seasons". Each season typically lasts for 2 weeks of competitive gameplay.

After a season has ended, there may be up to 1 week where the server remains online, however there is no competitive gameplay. This allows players to interact with each other in a more causal aspect as well as test out features and gameplay styles without risking a competitive disadvantage.

Speciality Seasons

On occasion, there will be a speciality season. These seasons usually correlate to holidays, may be shorter or longer than usual, and typically include limited-time exclusive content specific to the themed season. Speciality seasons usually include new temporary builds as well as more cosmetics.

Some examples of speciality seasons include Halloween and Christmas.

Ventox Map

The Ventox Map is made up of three individual worlds. Each of these worlds have a specific function and purpose.

Raiding World

The Raiding World is the default world that everyone spawns into when they first join Ventox. In this world, players can find Spawn. There are also Roads which extend from Spawn into the wild.

The Raiding World is also home to Team Bases as it's the only world where players can actually modify the landscape.

The Raiding World is completely reset to a brand new state for each season.

Spawn

Spawn is the first landmark that each player will see when they join Ventox for the first time. Spawn is a centralized safezone where players can interact with NPCs and Merchants as well as safely interact with other players. PvP is completely disabled in Spawn.

The Ventox Spawn is home to many of the most important Ventox functions.

  • Players can find Shop NPCs where they can buy and sell various items
  • Players can upgrade spawners at the Spawner Mason
  • Players can access quests at the Elite Quester
  • Players can purchase Team Crystals and purchase Team Cores
  • Players can redeem Banknotes & XP Bottles at the Banker
  • Players can access companions and stables

Beyond these important functions, Spawn is also home to a public Enchantment Room with maximized enchantment tables and anvils. If players are needing to repair their items or do some enchanting, this room is very important. It can be found in the Northwest Quadrant of Spawn.

Spawn also displays each of the server's Loot Boxes, where to obtain these boxes and what's inside of the each Loot Box.

Overall, Spawn is a critical "safezone" for players. Players should familize themselves with Spawn.

Roads

Roads are server-protected builds that extend from Spawn into the wild. While a player is on a road, they will get a Permanent Speed II effect. This helps players travel into and out of Spawn quickly.

Players need to be careful as roads are pvp-zones meaning that they are risky and leave you vulnerable for attack.

Bases

The Raiding World is the only world which players can directly modify by placing and destroying blocks. In doing so, the Raiding World is the only world where teams can create a base, hence it's the only world where players can also raid each other.

You can refer to the Raiding a Team Core Guide to assist you in how to raid a Team Core.

The Nexus

The Nexus is an Event World that hosts all the functional landmarks and in-game events for players to experience. The Nexus is the only world where players can fish, pan, brush, fight supply drops, kill bosses or capture the Summit.

The Nexus is primarily pvp-free, however there are certain regions and events that can enable pvp. On the scoreboard, there is an indicator to display whether or not you are vulnerable for attack.

Accessing The Nexus

Players can enter The Nexus by using /landmarks or /warps while in the Raiding World, at Spawn.

Leaving The Nexus

In order to leave The Nexus, players must find an Exit Traveller. These Exit Travellers are NPCs that can be found along pathways throughout The Nexus. Once an Exit Traveller has been found, a player can right-click on the NPC and confirm their exit. This will start a teleportation timer.

Once the teleportation has been completed, the player will be teleported back to the Raiding World, exactly to the point that they entered The Nexus from.

During this exit process, the player will glow leaving your vulnerable for attack and spotting. Be sure to check your surroundings for any hazards before attempting to leave The Nexus.

Nexus Landmarks

The Nexus lays host to many of the top gameplay mechanics on Ventox. The follow sub-section will explore these various landmarks.

Shady Shallows

The Shady Shallows is a custom fishing lake where players can fish for loot.

At the landmark, players will find a big lake where they can fish, some shop NPCs to buy and sell fishing-related items, as well as a competition leaderboard.

Shady Deposits

The Shady Deposits is a custom gold deposit where players can pan for loot.

At the landmark, players will find a big lake and underground cave where they can pan for treasures and gold. Players will also fine a Shop NPC to buy and sell panning-related items. There is also a competition leaderboard.

Shady Chambers

The Shady Chambers is a custom boss arena where players can fight a water-based boss.

At the landmark, players will find a big arena-style chmaber where a water-based boss can spawn.

Abandon Shafts

The Abandon Shafts is a custom mineshaft where players can mine for ores and unique loot.

At the landmark, players will find a big mining cavern where they can mine regenerating ores.

Pit of Heroes

The Pit of Heroes is a custom boss arena where players can fight a boss.

At the landmark, players will find a big arena-style pit where a boss can spawn.

The Summit

The Summit is a custom king of the hill event where players can fight to capture an always-active pvp landmark, including the rewards it holds.

At the landmark, players will find a large structure with a capture point.

Deserted Ruins

The Deserted Ruins is a custom excavation area where players can brush sand away for rare artifacts and unique loot.

At the landmark, players will find three individually-defined dig sites and a museum.

The Nether Cavern Portal

The Nether Cavern Portal is a Nether Portal that allows players to access The Nether Cavern. Before a player can access the nether, they must unlock access.

Pumpkin Paradise

The Pumpkin Paradise is a custom field and custom fishing lake, exclusive to the Halloween season, where players can compete to harvest the heaviest pumpkin and fish for loot.

At the landmark, players will find an open pumpkin patch where they can harvest pumpkins and a lake where players can fish. The landmark also is home to some shop NPCs to buy and sell pumpkin-related items. The landmark is only active when there is an on-going Pumpkin Weight Competition. There are no Sea Monsters or no weighted fishing competitions.

Sugar Rush Ridge

The Sugar Rush Ridge is a custom field, exclusive to the Christmas season, where players can compete to harvest the sweetest sugar cane.

At the landmark, players will find an open sugar cane field where they can harvest sugar cane as well as some shop NPCs to buy and sell sugar-related items. The landmark is only active when there is an on-going Sugar Cane Competition.

Snowdrift Shores

The Snowdrift Shores is a custom fishing lake, exclusive to the Christmas season, where players can fish for loot.

At the landmark, players will find a frozen lake where they can fish as well as some shop NPCs to buy and sell fishing-related items. There are no Sea Monsters or no weighted competitions. In order to fish at the Snowdrift Shores, players will require an Ice Pick.

Santa's Workshop

Santa's Workshop is a unique quest-based build, exclusive to the Christmas season, where players can find Mrs. Claus, Santa and Workshop Elves.

The Nether Cavern

The Nether Cavern is an Event World that hosts all the nether-based landmarks and nether-based in-game events for players to experience. The Nether Cavern is the only world where players can access nether-based functions as netherrite.

The Nether Cavern is a pvpzone meaning that it's risky to travel there and leaves you vulnerable for attack.

Accessing The Nether Cavern

In order to access The Nether Cavern, players must first unlock the related "Nether Access" Skill Perk. Once the Skill Perk has been unlocked, players can enter The Nexus. Players must find their way to The Nether Cavern Portal.

Once the player has reached The Nether Cavern Portal, they can interact with The Nether Guard. The Nether Guard will ask the player for:

  • $50,000
  • 1,000xp
  • 12x Amethyst Shards
  • 3x Emeralds

After the player pays The Nether Guard the required items to unlock The Nether Cavern, the player will be able to freely enter and exit The Nether Cavern. There is only one portal into and out of The Nether Cavern, so it's risky to travel there and leaves you vulnerable for attack.

Leaving The Nether Cavern

In order to leave The Nether Cavern, players must travel to a Nether Portal in The Nether Cavern to freely exit The Nether Cavern and return back into The Nexus.

The Nether Cavern Landmarks

The Nether Cavern lays host to many of the top gameplay mechanics on Ventox. The follow sub-section will explore these various landmarks.

Hellfire Hollows

The Hellfire Hollows is a custom mineshaft where players can mine for ores and unique loot, such as Ancient Debris and Glowstone.

At the landmark, players will find a big mining cavern where they can mine regenerating ores.

The Nether Portal

The Nether Portal is a Nether Portal that allows players to leave The Nether Cavern and re-enter The Nexus.

Tracking Landmarks

Players can track landmarks and points of interest in each of the worlds by using /landmarks or /warps.

Tracking in the Raiding World

When the player is in the Raiding World, players will be able to:

  • Track their Bed Location - if they have a bed
  • Track their Team Core - if they have a Team Core
  • Track Spawn
  • Teleport into The Nexus

Tracking in The Nexus

When the player is in The Nexus, players will be able to:

  • Track the Shady Shallows Fishing Lake
  • Track the Shady Deposits Gold Deposit
  • Track the Abandon Shafts Mineshaft
  • Track the Pit of Heroes Boss Arena
  • Track The Summit PvP Arena
  • Track the Deserted Ruins Excavation Area
  • Track The Nether Cavern Portal Nether Portal

In the Halloween season, players are able to:

  • Track the Pumpkin Pardise Fishing Lake and Pumpkin Patch

In the Christmas season, players are able to :

  • Track the Snowdrift Shores Fishing Lake
  • Track the Sugar Rush Ridge Sugar Cane Field

Tracking in The Nether Cavern

When the player is in The Nether Cavern, players will be able to:

  • Track the Hellfire Hollows Nether Mineshaft
